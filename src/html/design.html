Overview

Sleepless Nights is a 2D top down shooter web game. Either alone or in local multiplayer, the players must survive in a world where nightmares have come to life, using different weapons and attacks depending on their chosen characters.

Controls

Up to 2 players can play at once in local co-op, where both players use the keyboard. Player 1 uses the W, A, S, D keys to move and Spacebar to attack in the direction they are facing. Player 2 uses the Arrow keys to move, NumPad 0 to attack.

Gameplay

To win the game the players must navigate through the level and defeat a final boss at the end. Their progress will be impeded with enemies which must also be defeated. These enemies include small melee attackers, medium ranged attackers and a tougher boss enemy with both melee and ranged attacks.

Art and Visual Design

The art style for this game is cute and friendly, as to appeal to a younger audience. The player characters are 'chibi' style children with sprite models which describe their abilities. The enemies appear as nightmarish toys also with visual styles in accordance with their attacks or characteristics.

Level Design

The level design topology for this game is linear, such that the players must traverse from a start to a finish avoiding obstacles along the way. It is designed to look and feel like a regular modern house, with furniture scattered to act as movement obstacles. The level begins in a corridor attached to bedrooms, then moves to the stairs, living room, bathroom, kitchen and finally the garden outside. Enemies are spawned throughout the level at an increasing rate of difficulty depending on the enemy types and numbers.

Technical Design

This game has been developed using JavaScript with the Phaser 3 game framework. This allows game mechanics and features to be implemented into a website generated with HTML and CSS. As such the JetBrains Webstorm development environment was used to bring these systems together, providing an efficient workflow for implementing the game foundations and structure.
